Chika is a panel-by-panel comic reader. Open a CBZ or CBR file and Chika detects the panels on each
page with an on-device machine-learning model, then guides you through them one tap at a time:
page, then panel by panel, then back out to the whole page before turning to the next one. It turns
pinch-and-squint full-page reading into a comfortable, guided experience on a phone.

Features:

* Reads CBZ and CBR archives, including RAR5.
* On-device panel detection — a small neural model finds panels and speech balloons. Fully offline;
  nothing is uploaded.
* Smart grouping — tiny adjacent panels are merged into one comfortable zoom, and oversized panels
  are split with a bubble-aware cut.
* Reader controls — tap to step through panels, swipe to turn pages, pinch and drag to pan, a page
  scrubber, a "show whole page" button, and a left-to-right / right-to-left (manga) toggle.
* Library — import comics from your device, cover thumbnails, and resume exactly where you left off
  (page and panel).
* No accounts, no ads, no trackers, no network access.

Chika is free and open source (Mozilla Public License 2.0).
